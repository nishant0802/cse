New Life and Naming Ceremonies

New life and naming ceremonies offer an opportunity to mark the arrival of a new baby, but are also appropriate for any significant point of life change, such as adoption, change of gender or separation or divorce.
I am open to any and all enquiries

Mini-ceremonies...

As with weddings, these ceremonies might include such mini-ceremonies as oathing stones, mixing sands, loving cups, wishing trees and tree planting, and unity candles.

Why a naming ceremony...

Many people who are not religious feel they want to celebrate in a similar way to a christening, but without the religious aspect. It also offers a beautiful way to welcome older adopted or step-children. It is possible to appoint Godparents/ Guideparents/ Supporting Adults as part of the ceremony.

Transition...

A naming ceremony can be a fantastic way to tell important people in your life your new chosen name, celebrate this exciting step and say you would like them to use your new name and prefered pronouns from this point forwards.
